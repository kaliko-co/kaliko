## Mordants for Natural Dyes

This comprehensive ebook covers all the basic info about mordanting natural fibres for the plant dyeing process. I share my take on metal mordants, the way I approach it in my personal practice and professional work, as well as the recipes I use and recommend. I also included a chapter about alternatives to metal salts and useful tips for making patterns with mordants.

This ebook will be great for dyers who want to go deeper with their practice and understand how lasting colours are made.

Please note: In this publication, I talk about mordants only and did not include scouring, extracting and dyeing info, nor any plant dyeing recipes.

This ebook is over 70 pages, including many photographs.

**Table of contents:**

- Setting an objective
- The impact of mordants
- What are mordants
- Mordants in my process
- How to mordant properly
- Playing with mordants
- Alternative processes

\+ Mordanting recipes

If you're a beginner, please have a look at my Pocket Guide to Natural Dyeing.

## Pocket Guide to Natural Dyeing

This illustrated ebook covers all the basic info about mordanting natural fibres and dyeing with plants. A short and sweet summary on all the dyeing process steps, two basic mordanting recipes, and useful extraction tips.

This ebook will be great for beginners who want a quick and compact look into the world of plant dyeing, but will not serve you if you are looking for in-depth, step-by-step dye recipes. Suitable for both wool and cotton projects.

This ebook is 10 pages long, excluding the cover.

**Table of contents:**

- Planning
- Scouring
- Mordanting
- Soy milk
- Extracting
- Kitchen waste
- Dyeing

In addition to the ebook, you will also receive a PDF printout with the same content split into two sheets. Print double-sided and fold into two pocket-size booklets.

For in-depth info about mordanting, see my Mordants for Natural Dyes ebook.

## Dye Garden Manual

Dyeing with plants is an old craft which is becoming popular again. I got into botanical dyeing and have been experimenting with local fresh plants a lot since then. After trying so many plants from my local park, I decided to go a step further and start my own Dye Garden Project.

This digital project manual will provide you with the following:

- Requirements for the seeds to germinate and grow
- Cultivation tips
- Harvesting tips
- Recipes for extracting pigments from plants

**Which plants are covered?** Seven popular dye plants with great dyeing potential (hardiness zone in brackets):

- Chamomile (3–7)
- Weld (5–9)
- Coreopsis (6–9)
- Marigold (6–9)
- Madder (5–9)
- Hollyhock (3–9)
- Woad (6–9)

All plants can survive in hardiness zones 6–7, most of them tolerate zones 6–9. Please check your climate zone before purchasing.

The plants I chose can get you a full rainbow of colours after dyeing and overdyeing fabrics.
